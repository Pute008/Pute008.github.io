What theme park should you visit when you travel to England?

Stonehenge

Stonehenge is an ancient settlement from the stone age that lies around 13 kilometers north from Salisbury in Wiltshire, England. It’s a group of big rocks that have been there for centuries. They estimate that they were placed there around the stone age. Scientists have not found out why the rocks have been placed there, but they think it was used for religious purposes. Some people theorize that it was used for astrology.

People all over the world travel to Stonehenge to get a glimpse of one of England's oldest settlements. They are open from 09:30 - 18:30. It costs £25 to enter and see Stonehenge. If you want to see it for yourself, then you can check out this link.

Big Ben

Big Ben is a nickname given to the big clock that is located on the parliament building Westminster Palace. The tower is 97,5 meters tall with a square base of 144 M2. It was later given its name Elizabeth Tower after Queen Elisabeth II 60 year anniversary. The tower had previously had a less known name, St. Stephens Tower.

They are open from 09:00 - 16:00 in October to June and if you want to enter Big Ben you would have to pay around £30. If you want to book a tour then you should click this link.

London Eye

If you are looking for a theme park that you want to go to in England, you should definitely go to the London Eye. It took seven years and £75 million to build the London Eye. Construction started in 1998. Many parts were built off site and shipped to the construction site where it was assembled. It opened on December 31, 1999. It has a capacity for around 25 people each in every glass gondola, which approximately can have a maximum 800 people. The lap time is 30 minutes so you have a good time to watch over all the beautiful architecture in London

They are open from 11 - 18 every day. A ticket costs around £29 - £42. If you want to have a ride then you should click this link.
